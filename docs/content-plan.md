## Editorial Principles

- Lead with outcomes first: what changed, improved, shipped, automated, reduced, increased, or clarified.
- Use concrete proof: metrics, links, screenshots, repos, demos, architecture diagrams, before/after notes.
- Avoid unsupported claims such as “highly scalable,” “passionate,” or “expert” unless backed by examples.
- Keep copy recruiter-friendly: short sections, strong headings, fast skim value.
- Do not invent employers, roles, metrics, projects, or technology choices. Use placeholders until verified.

## Site Copy Structure

### Home Hero

**Goal:** Tell a recruiter or hiring manager who Himanshu is, what he builds, and why they should keep reading.

Recommended structure:

1. **Headline**
   - Format: `[TARGET ROLE] who builds [PRIMARY VALUE / PRODUCT TYPE] with [TOP SKILL AREA]`.
   - Example placeholder: `Software Engineer focused on [DOMAIN], [TECH], and measurable product impact.`
2. **Subheadline**
   - 1-2 sentences covering current role/background, target role, strongest skills, and proof.
   - Example placeholder: `I am [CURRENT ROLE] with experience in [SKILL 1], [SKILL 2], and [SKILL 3]. My work includes [PROJECT TYPE], [PROJECT TYPE], and [PROJECT TYPE], with outcomes like [METRIC] and [METRIC].`
3. **Primary calls to action**
   - `View Projects`
   - `Read Blog`
   - `Download Resume`
   - `Contact Me`
4. **Proof strip**
   - Short facts only after verification: `[YEARS EXPERIENCE]`, `[KEY TECH]`, `[PROJECT COUNT]`, `[CERTIFICATION]`, `[OPEN SOURCE / LIVE DEMO]`.

### About Section

**Goal:** Give enough context to build trust without repeating the resume.

Recommended structure:

1. **Opening identity paragraph**
   - Current/target role, core technical lane, and professional direction.
2. **What Himanshu is strongest at**
   - 3-5 bullets tied to real examples.
   - Example: `[SKILL]: used in [PROJECT] to achieve [RESULT].`
3. **How he works**
   - Communication, ownership, debugging, learning habits, collaboration style.
4. **Career direction**
   - What roles he is targeting in the next job switch and why.
5. **Personal note, if useful**
   - Optional and brief: learning focus, community, writing, side projects.

### Resume Section

**Goal:** Make the resume easy to access and preview.

Recommended structure:

1. **Short intro**
   - `Looking for [TARGET ROLE] opportunities where I can contribute to [DOMAIN / PRODUCT TYPE].`
2. **Resume download/view link**
   - Link to hosted PDF once available.
3. **Highlights**
   - 4-6 bullets pulled from verified resume content.
   - Each bullet should include skill + project/work context + result.
4. **Availability/contact note**
   - Location/time zone, preferred contact method, job preferences if public.

### Project Writeup Template

Use this structure for every project page/card.

1. **Project title**
   - `[PROJECT NAME] — [SHORT VALUE STATEMENT]`
2. **One-line summary**
   - `Built [WHAT] for [USER / PROBLEM] using [TECH], resulting in [IMPACT].`
3. **Problem**
   - What pain point existed? Who had it? Why did it matter?
4. **Role**
   - Himanshu's exact ownership: solo builder, team member, backend/frontend/full-stack, deployment, testing, documentation, architecture, etc.
5. **Tech**
   - Languages, frameworks, databases, APIs, infrastructure, CI/CD, testing tools.
6. **Approach**
   - 3-5 concise bullets describing the most important engineering decisions.
7. **Impact**
   - Metrics, user outcome, performance improvement, saved time, reliability gain, learning outcome, or shipped artifact.
8. **Links**
   - GitHub repo, live demo, case study, screenshots, video walkthrough, related blog post.
9. **What I would improve next**
   - Shows judgment and maturity: testing, security, performance, UX, observability, deployment, docs.

### Contact Section

**Goal:** Make the next step obvious.

Recommended structure:

1. **Direct invitation**
   - `If you are hiring for [TARGET ROLE], I would be glad to talk.`
2. **Best channels**
   - Email, LinkedIn, GitHub, resume link.
3. **Role fit note**
   - Target job titles, preferred locations/remote status, notice period if public.
4. **Optional collaboration note**
   - Open source, freelance, speaking, mentoring, or writing interests only if true.

## Blog Content Strategy

### Theme 1: Skill Deep-Dives

1. `How I Approach Building [TYPE OF APPLICATION] with [TECH STACK]`
2. `What I Learned While Improving [PERFORMANCE / RELIABILITY / UX] in [PROJECT]`
3. `A Practical Guide to Structuring [FRONTEND / BACKEND / FULL-STACK] Projects`
4. `Debugging [PROBLEM TYPE]: A Step-by-Step Breakdown from [PROJECT]`

### Theme 2: Project Case Studies

5. `Building [PROJECT NAME]: From Problem Statement to Shipped Version`
6. `How [PROJECT NAME] Solved [USER / BUSINESS PROBLEM]`
7. `The Technical Decisions Behind [PROJECT NAME]`
8. `What I Would Rebuild Differently in [PROJECT NAME]`

### Theme 3: Job-Switch Portfolio Posts

9. `How I Am Preparing My Developer Portfolio for a [TARGET ROLE] Job Switch`
10. `Turning Project Work into Recruiter-Friendly Case Studies`
11. `The Skills I Want My Portfolio to Prove for [TARGET ROLE] Roles`
12. `My [NUMBER]-Week Plan to Become Stronger at [TARGET SKILL]`

### Theme 4: Lessons Learned / How-Tos

13. `Lessons from Shipping [PROJECT TYPE] as a [ROLE]`
14. `How I Document Technical Tradeoffs in My Projects`
15. `From Local Project to Live Demo: Deploying [PROJECT] with [HOSTING / CI TOOL]`

## Publishing Recommendations

- Start with 3 strong project case studies before publishing many general posts.
- For every technical post, link to at least one project, repo, or demo when possible.
- Use a consistent ending: `What I learned`, `What I would improve`, and `Where to see the code`.
- Prioritize quality and proof over volume. A recruiter should learn something concrete from every post.

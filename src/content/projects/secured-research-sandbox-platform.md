## Case study

Security research platforms have a different risk profile from typical internal developer environments. Researchers need speed and self-service, but the environments must remain strongly isolated, especially when they are used for malware analysis or other security research.

At Microsoft Security, I work on the Research Efficiency Platform, which enables security researchers to provision secured, ephemeral sandboxes in a self-service way. These environments are kept isolated from other development and production tenants. My work includes building platform features that streamline researcher workflows while maintaining security posture and tenant isolation.

I also contribute to multicloud secured Kubernetes infrastructure. The clusters are delivered with pre-built infrastructure components so customers can run their workflows on them, with observability, scalability, and resiliency built in.

This work brings together platform engineering, Kubernetes, cloud infrastructure, and security operations. It is focused on making secure infrastructure easier to consume without weakening the controls that keep research environments isolated.
